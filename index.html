Violinist Adam


Adam Chin began studying the violin at age 4 using a ruler and a tissue box with rubber bands stretched about it. He began his studies at the Preparatory School of the New England Conservatory of Music (NEC) a month later, and at 7 began the piano as a second instrument. At 12, Adam served as concertmaster of the summer orchestra at Harvard University, and became one of the youngest members of the Youth Philharmonic Orchestra at NEC and the Senior (Greater) Boston Youth Symphony Orchestra.

At 15, he completed the NEC Preparatory School Certificate Program with highest honors at each of the four levels, and performed on the national radio show From the Top where he also demonstrated his ability to recite from memory several hundred digits of pi. Adam has participated in numerous music festivals, including the annual contemporary and chamber music festivals at NEC, Greenwood, and the Heifetz International Music Institute. Adam is also an avid chamber musician. His chamber groups have been prize-winners at the International Chamber Music Ensemble Competition, and in 2008, he won the Eugene Lehner Chamber Music Award.

Adam studied chamber music with Rodney Lister, and violin with James Buswell, Lynn Chang, Magdalena Richter, and Melba Sandberg.
